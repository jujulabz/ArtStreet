Why Us?

We are more than just a website we are an initiative. Our goal is to bring art to you, not only online but also in person through community events and workshops. These workshops will provide a space for artists of all levels to come together, learn new techniques, share ideas, and collaborate on creative projects. As we continue to grow, we are committed to refining our mission to better serve our audience. In the meantime, enjoy our showcase, discover new artists, learn about art galleries near you, and most importantly, have fun!

Aminah Robinsion

Aminah Brenda Lynn Robinson (1940–2015) was a renowned artist from Columbus, Ohio, celebrated for her mixed-media works that explored African American history, culture, and storytelling. Her art often incorporated found objects, textiles, and handmade paper, creating intricate pieces she called "RagGonNons," which were designed to evolve over time. Robinson's legacy continues to inspire, and she was honored with numerous accolades, including a MacArthur "Genius" Grant in 2004.

April Sunami

April Sunami is a mixed-media artist from Columbus, Ohio, whose work prominently features Black women as central figures, exploring themes of race, identity, and representation. Her creations have been exhibited across the U.S., as well as internationally in Ghana and Cuba, showcasing her cultural and artistic impact. With degrees in Art History from Ohio University and Ohio State University, Sunami combines innovative materials like fabric and glass to add depth and meaning to her powerful and symbolic artwork.
